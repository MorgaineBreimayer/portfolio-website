A web-based retail app

UX/UI Case Study

Project Overview

What is Macra.me?

Macra.me is a web-based retail app that gives users access to a large, well-curated array of woven arts products. The web app gives users the ability to have products delivered to any address, try products risk-free in their own homes by offering free return shipping. It saves the user the time and energy of shopping in a physical store.

Objective

An easy-to-use shopping experience that helps the user find products uniquely suited to their needs and tastes.

Deliverables & Role

My role as UI designer was:

  • Create a brand guideline for the web app
  • Based on the user stories, create a user flow diagram to solve for user needs
  • Low & mid fidelity wireframes
  • User testing results
  • High fidelity screens
  • Responsive design

Logo Guidelines

Logo Usage

Our logo mimics the intricate patterns found in macrame, replicated using thin, clean lines for clear visibility. The font used is Bellerose Light, whose strong geometric influences and flourishes parallel the geometry found in macrame patterns.

The logo consists of two elements, the logomark and the logotype, both of which can be used individually.

Let it breathe

Just like a wall hanging, our logo looks best when given a bit of breathing room. Leave padding of 1/2 the width of the logomark on all sides for the best viewing experience.

Brand Guidelines

Guiding Principles

  1. Hand-crafted with love
    The tradition of macrame has been passed down through generations. As we create, we pass on these traditions & imbue our products with our deep love for woven arts.
  2. Home is where the art is
    We aim to fill people’s lives with cozy textiles that will create a warm, welcoming atmosphere in their home through a bohemian aesthetic.
  3. Individuality is beautiful
    Every one of our products is free spirited & individual, just like our users. We hope seeing our art on a daily basis will inspire our users to make the world more beautiful in the ways that only they can.

Writing Style

Our writing style is friendly and warm. Since our users are artistic, free spirited types, we run against the grain of bland corporate speak.

Imagery

Our app uses photography to showcase our products. Product photography is bright and clear, with light backgrounds to contrast the product. Color palettes should be earth tones.

Context photography is used to give the user and idea of how our products look in the real world. They should depict people enjoying spaces decorated with our products or our products being incorporated into home decor, preferably featuring plants, wood, & other natural materials.

User Flow

User Stories for MVP

As a new customer, I want to access the inventory without having to register, so that I can make sure this store has what I’m looking for before having to create an account.

As a customer, I want to be able to place multiple items in a shopping cart, so that I can purchase more than one item at a time.

As a customer, I want there to be a variety of payment options, so that I can choose the payment method that suits me best.

User Testing

User 1

Age: 32Gender: female
Shops online: every other day

Feedback: I didn’t like that when I clicked “keep shopping” it took me back to the homepage, I’d rather it keep me on the original item page.

Solution: Rearrange the userflow to have the “keep shopping” link direct the user back to the product page.

User 2

Age: 26Gender: female
Shops online: several times a week

Feedback: I was really confused by the layout of the wall hanging page because it was side scroll & I could only see the small section. I love the side menu, I hate when online shops don’t have that.

Solution: By decreasing the number of horizontal scroll rows from two to one, the next section of the page will appear above the fold.

User 3

Age: 37Gender: nonbinary
Shops online: daily

Feedback: I want the “keep shopping” link to return me to the page I was on, not the homepage. also, I didn’t know which color was selected on the product page.

Solution: To solve the color swatches problem, there will have to be a separate UI style for the selected item.

Retrospective

What went well

The color scheme came out really nice in this project, subtle but very in touch with the products. I broke with tradition for the modal design, giving it a diamond shape instead of a rectangle, and it was well received. The navigation for the web app was universally praised in user testing.

Challenges overcome

Since this project focused early on building brand guidelines, I put extra energy into getting the logo right. The trick was making something that looked good even at small sizes. I went through multiple iterations that were too complex, I struggled with finding the right balance between reflecting the intricacies of the shop products while remaining minimal and contemporary. Getting feedback from my peers was crucial to this process.
