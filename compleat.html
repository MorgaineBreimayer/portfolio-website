ComplEat Case Study

You already have everything you need

UX/UI Case Study

What is ComplEat?

ComplEat is a web-based app that helps users find recipes
based on ingredients they already have in their kitchen.

ComplEat is a web-based app that helps users find recipes
based on ingredients they already have in their kitchen.

Hypothesis

Everyone has the need to find recipes for specific ingredients.

From a desire to not waste food, to working with a new ingredient, to not having made it to the grocery store, people need access to tested recipes to help them start cooking when they have food they specifically want to use.

User Research

To identify and connect with a target audience, I conducted a series of user interviews and asked about their needs and cooking behavior.

The interviews revealed a unanimous need for the app, even before the specific app idea was presented to them.

The results helped me identify which features potential users consider absolutely necessary in a recipe app.

User Personas

Who is the user?

Cooks of all skill levels |

User Personas

The data from the user interviews was synthesized into User Personas to provide key insights into users’ needs and how they will use the app. The User Personas helped maintain focus while developing solutions for them.

User Stories

By building User Stories I was able to define the jobs to be done and weigh which features were necessary for a minimum viable product.

User Story 1: When I enter the web app, I want to be able to enter ingredients that I already have available to me.
Feature requirements: clickable pantry buttons

User Story 2: When I enter my ingredients, I want the app to remember what I have entered for next time
Feature requirements: account creation

User Story 3: When I have search results, I want to be able to filter them by diet, meal type, or specific ingredient.
Feature requirements: advanced search filters

User Story 4: When I find a recipe I like, I want to be able to save the recipe.
Feature requirements: account creation, saved recipes section of web app, clickable save button on recipes

User Flow & IA Map

After defining the objectives of the app, I created a user flow that focused on specific tasks the user would need to complete.

Mood Boards

I created two different mood boards to explore the visual direction of the app. I selected this board because it is practical, inspires cooking confidence, and applies to all food groups.

Wireframe Progression

Using the user flows I’d established, I went through a round of rapid paper protoyping. These were turned into low-fidelity wireframes. I then applied the visual direction of the mood board to the wiireframes to bring the app to life.

Retrospective

What went well

Narrowing down the list of MVP features was crucial for the success of this app. I had a fairly long list of dream features, and user testing contributed even more ideas. Sitting down with my mentor to go through the feature list and determine which were necessary for the MVP made the scope fo the project much more manageable.

Challenges overcome

While I was creating the low fidelity wireframes and starting to think about the visual direction of the app, I felt pulled in two very different directions. Having the mood board assignment right after was the perfect way to work out which direction was the right one.

What could be improved

Next time I would choose standard screen sizes for my break points in responsive design, since I ended up having to resize all my wireframes in order to make mockups that fit the screens. I’d also make the medium break point a horizontal layout instead of vertical. For creating my mockup, I would start with a rough drawing on paper to help me visualize the final result and guide me as I built the pdf so I am not so overwhelmed by the empty page.
